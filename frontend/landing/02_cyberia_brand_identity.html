01
The Platform

Mission, vision,
and the four
commitments.

Every decision in this document derives from the four statements below. When the system is ambiguous — when a designer or writer must choose between two plausible paths — the platform is the tiebreaker.

Mission
To build the settlement layer that allows open-source work to flow at the speed of the economy it powers.
Vision
A world where contribution is a first-class economic event — measured, rewarded, and settled on neutral rails.
Promise
Every claim this network makes is verifiable on-chain or in the open repository. If you cannot verify it, we did not say it.
Position
Cyberia is not a product you use. It is infrastructure you build on. A public good with durable economics.
i.
Sovereignty over spectacle.
The network and its participants remain self-determined. We decline permissioning, hype cycles, and gatekeepers — including our own. Every component is open, forkable, and independently operable.
ii.
Proof over promise.
Roadmaps describe what is being built, not what will be sold. Claims link to code. Numbers link to transactions. The brand's credibility compounds through verification, not marketing.
iii.
Contribution, not consumption.
Cyberia favors participants over audiences. The design of every touchpoint — from documentation to interface — assumes the reader may one day become a contributor.
iv.
Durable over viral.
We optimize for being alive in a decade, not for being trending this week. Growth that requires betraying the thesis is not growth we pursue.

The Type System

Three voices:
editorial, functional,
technical.

Fraunces for display — a contemporary serif with a literary, slightly mechanical personality. Inter Tight for body and UI — a geometric sans with the legibility of Helvetica and the precision of modern display faces. JetBrains Mono for all technical content.

The Voice

A senior
developer writing
release notes.

The voice is editorial, not promotional. Read it aloud and it should sound like someone with nothing to prove telling you, precisely, what they built. It is the voice of a system that has earned its confidence.

"
Show the work.
Every claim arrives with evidence attached. A commit hash, a contract address, a block number, a link to live code. If a sentence cannot be verified, it does not appear. This is not a stylistic preference — it is an ethical one.
"
Stay level.
No exclamation marks. No superlatives. No "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "next-generation." The tone remains flat regardless of the news. Good launches do not need to be sold as great. The work is the claim.
"
Invite inspection.
We write as if every reader will check our math. Linking to source, commits, and explorers is a reflex, not an afterthought. The tone assumes the reader is as smart as we are — possibly smarter — and will not thank us for simplification.

Elevator Pitches · By length
◆ 10 Words
A settlement layer for open-source work. Live on mainnet.
◆ 50 Words
Cyberia is a public Layer 1 blockchain that makes open-source contribution a native on-chain event. Stars, commits, and pull requests settle as verifiable transactions. Every line of code is open, every decision is logged, and every claim is provable. Infrastructure for the economy that software built.
◆ 200 Words
Open source runs the world. The people who build it are rarely paid. That gap — between the value created and the value returned — is a structural failure, not a problem of goodwill. Cyberia is an attempt to close it.

Cyberia is a public, EVM-compatible Layer 1 blockchain operating as the settlement layer for open-source work. Contributions — code, community, capital — are recorded, verified, and rewarded as native on-chain events. The first rails for this are already live: automated airdrop settlement for engagement on GitHub, Telegram, and X.

The network is being built entirely in public. Every commit is live-streamed. The entire stack is GPL-3.0. There is no private repository and no off-the-record roadmap. Near-term, the team is shipping core infrastructure: DEX, lending primitives, a contribution-weighted governance system, and a bridge to Solana.

Cyberia is not optimizing for short-term attention. It is optimizing for being alive in 2036, running real contributor economies, and serving as neutral rails for every network that needs them.
